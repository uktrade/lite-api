Authorisation
The Secretary of State authorises the person named in box 1 of this licence ("the licensee") to export or transfer the goods, software and/or technology described in box 14 ("the listed items") to the person named in box 5, before the end of the date shown in box 3, subject to the conditions attached or specified in this licence as set out below.
Authorisation for the goods, software and/or technology has been granted under Article 26 of the Export Control Order 2008, as amended and Article 9 of Council Regulation (EC) No. 428/2009, as amended.
Conditions
1.This condition applies if listed items are being exported from the United Kingdom to a destination outside the territory of the Member States of the European Community. The licensee must quote the number of this licence to the proper officer of HM Revenue and Customs when the listed items are presented to that officer for export or must comply with such alternative arrangements as are agreed with that officer.
2.This condition applies if listed items are being exported from the United Kingdom to a destination in the territory of a Member State of the European Community:
a.The licensee must quote the number of this licence to the proper officer of HM Revenue and Customs at least three working days before the date of the proposed export identifying a location where the listed items will be capable of inspection at any time during those three days, or shall comply with such alternative arrangements as are agreed with an officer of HM Revenue and Customs. This paragraph does not apply where the licensee is exporting firearms covered by EC Directive 91/477/EEC or ammunition for use therewith.
b.The licensee must:
i. maintain records of all transactions carried out under this licence,
ii. present a copy of these records each time the licence is presented to HM Revenue & Customs,
iii. permit these records to be inspected and copied by any person authorised by the Secretary of State or the Commissioners, and
iv. inform the Export Control Organisation ("ECO") when the licence becomes exhausted.
3.This condition applies if listed items are being exported or transferred from a Member State of the European Community other than the United Kingdom. The licensee must present a hard copy of this licence, signed and stamped by an official of the Department for Business, Innovation and Skills, to the appropriate authorities in that Member State on request.
4. This condition applies if listed items (being software or technology) have been transferred. The licensee must keep records that contain sufficient detail to allow the following to be identified:
a. A description of the listed items that have been transferred;
b. The date(s) of the transfer(s); and
c. In so far as it is known to the licensee, the name and address of the end-user of the listed items.
The licensee must permit these records to be inspected and copied by any person authorised by the Secretary of State or the Commissioners.
For listed items covered by Regulation (EU) 258/2012, marking details and serial numbers must form part of the supporting commercial documentation accompanying the export.

Standard conditions
1.This licence shall not affect a prohibition or restriction in any legislation other than the legislation under which this licence was issued.
2. This licence is not transferable
3. This licence may be modified or revoked at any time by the Secretary of State
4. This licence is valid when printed only if signed and stamped by an official of the Department for Business, Innovation and Skills.
General Notes
Details of any change to export control legislation can be found at
https://www.gov.uk/business-and-industry/export-controls
The box numbers used on the licence form follow those in the model annexed to Council Regulation (EC) No. 428/2009.
Unless the context otherwise requires, any expression used in this licence shall have the meaning it bears in the Export Control Order 2008. However, the definition of "transfer" in that Order shall apply as if for the words "except in articles 10 and 11" to the end of the definition there were substituted the words "or a Member State of the European Community specified as a country of origin in box 8 of this licence".
Warning: Failure to comply with any conditions attaching to this licence may lead to forfeiture of the goods and/or to prosecution under the Customs and Excise Management Act 1979, or the legislation under which this licence was issued.
